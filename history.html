from first discovery to first summit ― and beyond.

Standing as the 2nd tallest mountain in the world, K2 is renowned for its towering height as well as challenging climbing conditions, attracting many climbers over the years.
The rich history of K2 stretches all the way back to its discovery in 1856, to its first summit in 1954, to today.

1856

K2 is first surveyed by the British Great Trigonometric Survey of India. Thomas Montgomerie, who was part of the survey, assigns the name K2, standing for "Karakoram 2."

1902

Oscar Eckenstein leads a 5-man team on the first serious attempt to climb K2, reaching 6,525m (21,407 ft) using the Northeast Ridge before complications arose which made them turn back.

1909

An expedition led by Prince Luigi Amadeo, Duke of the Abruzzi, reaches an elevation of 6,250m (20,510 ft) on the South East Spur, known now as the Abruzzi Spur.

1938

An American expedition, led by Charles Houston, attempts K2. They reach around 7,925 meters (26,000 feet) on the Abruzzi Spur but are forced to turn back due to storms.

1939

An American expedition led by Fritz Wiessner with six teammates set a new altitude record on the Abruzzi Spur by reaching 8,382 m (27,500 ft).

1953

Another American team led by Dr. Charles Houston reached 7,900 m on the Abruzzi Spur. One team member, Art Gilkey, was lost in an avalance due to altitude-realted health complications.

1954

On July 31, members Achille Compagnoni and Lino Lacedelli of an Italian expedition become the first people to stand on the summit, ascending via the Abruzzi Spur.

1978

Reinhold Messner and Louis Lacedelli achieve the first summit of K2 without using supplementary oxygen, showing it was possible, albeit extremely difficult.

1982

A Japanese team led by Isao Shinkai and Masatsugu Konishi were the first to summit K2 from the north side, without using bottled oxygen.

1986

Polish climber Wanda Rutkiewicz becomes the first woman to summit K2, on June 23 1986. Her climb came in a tragic season where 13 others perished, making her feat even more significant.

2021

Originally considered impossible due to extreme and unpredictable weather, a team of Nepali climbers led by Nirmal Purja made history by completing the first summit of K2 during winter.

other notable facts ―

the belay

The "Schoening Belay" refers to a legendary act of heroism by climber Pete Schoening during a 1953 American expedition on K2. Using an ice axe and rope, he single-handedly saved six climbers from falling to their deaths, an act that has become one of the most famous rescues in mountaineering history.

ghosts

Legends of ghosts on K2 are tied to its dangerous reputation. Some climbers claim to have felt eerie presences, a testament to the psychological strain that comes with attempting such a deadly ascent.

k2 taller than everest?

Base​d​ on new satellite data, rumors circulated that K2 was actually slightly higher than Everest. Italian Professor Ardito Desio organized surveys of K2 and Everest, reaffirming Everest’s superiority.

unconquered routes

Despite many successful ascents, K2 still has several unclimbed routes, including some on the mountain's east face and the notorious southeast face. These untouched routes present extreme technical challenges, with vertical rock walls, avalanches, and unpredictable weather making them some of the hardest in the world.

the savage mountain

K2 is often called the "Savage Mountain" due to its harsh conditions and deadly climbing routes. With steep slopes, unpredictable weather, and a high fatality rate, it's earned this ominous title.

first ski descent

The first ski descent of K2 was made by Andrzej Bargiel in 2018, marking a historic moment. He successfully skied down the entire mountain without using supplementary oxygen.
